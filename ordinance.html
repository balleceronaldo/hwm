Section 1 – Title

This ordinance shall be known as An Ordinance Establishing a Local Hazardous Waste Program, Providing Guidelines for the Handling, Storage, Collection, Transport, Treatment and Disposal of Hazardous Waste in the City of Baguio.

Section 2 – Declared Policy

The city recognizes the vital importance of protecting public health and the environment from the adverse effects of hazardous waste. It shall implement the proper handling, collection, storage, transport, treatment, and disposal of hazardous wastes through a local management program. This program shall prioritize the management of household hazardous wastes (HHW) and hazardous wastes generated by small-scale sources—such as ambulant vendors, small eateries (e.g., 4-seater establishments), and other micro-enterprises—which, while governed by the standards of RA 6969, require localized collection and consolidation by the City to ensure environmentally sound disposal through accredited facilities.

Section 3 – Scope and Coverage

This Ordinance shall cover the entire territorial jurisdiction of the City of Baguio. It shall apply to households, city government offices, and small-scale sources that produce the following hazardous wastes:

  • Arsenic and its compounds (Waste Number D402)
  • Lead compounds (Waste Number D406)
  • Mercury and mercury compounds (Waste Number D407)
  • Solvent Based (Waste Number F601)
  • Grease wastes (Waste Number H802)
  • Used industrial oil including sludge (Waste Number I101)
  • Vegetable Oil including sludge (Waste Number I102)
  • Tallow (Waste Number I103)
  • Container previously containing toxic chemical substances (Waste Number J201)
  • Pharmaceuticals and drugs (Waste Number M503)
  • Pesticides (Waste Number M504)
  • Waste Electrical and electronic equipment (Waste Number M506)
  • Special Wastes (Waste Number M507)

Section 4 – Definition of Terms

Key terms used in this ordinance include:

  • Busted Fluorescent Lamps – damaged or non-functional lamps containing mercury compounds.
  • Clustered Hazardous Waste Management – collective arrangement by small generators for transport and disposal.
  • Hazardous Wastes – substances posing unreasonable risk to health and environment.
  • Household Hazardous Waste (HHW) – hazardous waste from residential sources.
  • Small Quantity Generator – generates between 5,000 and 250,000 kg/year of hazardous waste.
  • Waste Electrical and Electronic Equipment (E-waste) – discarded electronic devices.

(Full definitions available in the official ordinance document.)

Section 5 – Hazardous Waste Management

Hazardous waste management comprises methods to minimize or eliminate effects on human health and the environment, including minimization, collection, transportation, treatment, and disposal.

Section 6 – Hazardous Waste Collection and Transport

Collection shall avoid public nuisance and include:

  • Establishment of barangay collection points and schedules
  • Hiring of accredited transporters by small-scale generators
  • Collection and transport of e-waste to authorized facilities

Section 7 – Hazardous Waste Storage, Treatment and Disposal

The City Government shall operate temporary storage facilities, coordinate with agencies, and comply with national regulations.

Section 8 – Database Management

The City shall maintain a Hazardous Waste Information Database accessible to the public, including directories, guidelines, collection locations, and data-sharing with DENR.

Section 9 – Role of Small Quantity Generator and Micro-Enterprise Generator

Responsibilities include registration, segregation, labeling, manifesting, and safe storage.

Section 10 – Role of the City Environment and Parks Management Office (CEPMO)

CEPMO shall secure permits, monitor, conduct IEC campaigns, operate facilities, collect waste, and perform related functions.

Section 11 – Role of the Barangay Officials

Barangays shall conduct IEC, establish collection schedules, submit reports, and install signage.

Section 12 – Collection of Electronic Waste Outside of Baguio City

Non-residents shall pay ₱30.00/kg processing fee; 30% share allocated to participating barangays.

Section 13 – Disposal of Electronic Waste from Local Government Units

Other LGUs must enter into a Memorandum of Agreement for disposal.

Section 14 – Hazardous Waste Fees

Households and micro-enterprises shall pay monthly flat rates (schedule to be defined).

Section 15 – Prohibited Acts

Includes improper disposal, burning, unauthorized transport, and obstruction of collection points.

Section 16 – Penalties for Violations

First offense: ₱1,000
Second: ₱1,500
Third: ₱5,000 or 6-month business permit suspension.
Fines accrue to the General Fund.

Section 17 – Incentive

20% discount on city regulatory fees/taxes for complete manifest submission.

Section 18 – Awards and Recognition

“Luntiang Baguio Waste Warrior Award” for compliant generators.

Section 19 – Funding

₱1,000,000 initial appropriation from General Fund, incorporated annually in CEPMO budget.

Section 20 – Implementing Departments

CEPMO is the lead department, supported by various city offices.

Section 21 – Separability Clause

Invalidity of any provision shall not affect the remainder.

Section 22 – Effectivity Clause

Takes effect per Local Government Code provisions.
