Used Oil

Why hazardous? Pollutes water and soil severely.

How to dispose: Pour into sealed plastic containers; drop off at facility/events.

Busted Fluorescent Lamps (BFL)

Why hazardous? Contain mercury – toxic if released.

How to dispose: Wrap carefully or use a box; avoid breaking more.

E-Waste

Why hazardous? Contains lead, mercury, and other toxins.

How to dispose: Drop intact items at Bakakeng or events.

Expired Medicines

Why hazardous? Can contaminate water sources if flushed.

How to dispose: Bring in original packaging; use take-back programs.

Educational guide to common HHW in Philippine homes.
These are NOT all accepted yet – Store safely at home!

Batteries

Why hazardous? Heavy metals leach into soil/water.

Safe storage: Keep dry in a container; await future collections.

Paints, Thinners & Solvents

Why hazardous? Flammable and toxic VOCs.

Safe storage: Tight lids; ventilated area.

Household Cleaners

Why hazardous? Corrosive (bleach, ammonia).

Safe storage: Original containers; away from children.

Pesticides & Herbicides

Why hazardous? Highly toxic to humans/wildlife.

Safe storage: Sealed; locked cabinet.

Aerosol Cans

Why hazardous? Pressurized and flammable.

Safe storage: Do not puncture; cool place.

Use permanent facilities or join scheduled collection events (often at Malcom Square, barangays, or schools).
